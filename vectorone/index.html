Straight Line Motion

Important Equations and Definitions

Distance and Time

Distance = \(d\)

Time = \(t\)

Position

Position = \(X\), \(X_f\) (final), \(X_i\) (initial), \(X_0\) ("X nought")

Displacement = change in position: \( \Delta X = X_f - X_i \)

Speed and Velocity

Speed (\(v\)) = \( \frac{d}{t} \)

Velocity (\(V\)) (vector) = \( \frac{\Delta X}{t} = \frac{X_f - X_i}{t} \)

Slope-Intercept Form

Equation: \(y = mx + b\)

Equation of Final Position

\( X_f = Vt + X_i \)

Rearrange to solve for \(V\):

\( \frac{Vt}{t} = \frac{X_f - X_i}{t} \)

Therefore, \( V = \frac{\Delta X}{t} \)

Solving a Story Problem

A person starts at the 2m mark. At a velocity of -1m/s, they walk for 6 seconds. What is their final position?

Step-by-Step Solution

1. Write down all values (Givens):

\( X_i = 2\,m \)
\( V = -1\,m/s \)
\( t = 6\,s \)
\( X_f = ? \)

2. Write down the equation:

\( X_f = Vt + X_i \)

3. Plug in and solve:

\( X_f = (-1)(6) + 2 = -6 + 2 = -4 \)

4. Write out the answer:

Final position: \( X_f = -4\,m \)
