Simplifying Radical Expressions: A Guide

This guide will illustrate how to simplify different types of radical expressions:

Square Root of a Non-Perfect Square

  • √18

    Step 1: Factorize 18 into 2 * 9
    Step 2: Take square root of a factor(s) if possible. For 9, its square root is 3.
    Step 3: Write final answer as 3√2

Cube Root of a Non-Perfect Cube

  • ∛54

    Step 1: Factorize 54 into 3 * 3 * 6
    Step 2: Look for trios of the same number. For the factorized 54, we have one trio of 3’s.
    Step 3: Write final answer as 3∛6

Rationalizing the Denominator

  • 5/√2

    Step 1: Multiply numerator and denominator by √2
    Step 2: Simplify multiplication to get 5√2/2 as the final answer.
